The shortage is particularly acute in specialized areas:

- **ML engineering** — The bridge between data science and production systems. These professionals are rarer and more valuable than data scientists.
- **NLP & LLM specialists** — Demand has exploded with the rise of generative AI. Companies pay premiums for engineers who can fine-tune and deploy large language models.
- **Computer vision** — Critical for manufacturing, healthcare, retail, and financial services applications.
- **MLOps engineers** — The people who keep AI systems running in production. A relatively new discipline with a very thin talent pool.

### The domain premium

Generic AI talent is hard enough to find. But **domain-specific AI talent** — engineers who understand both the technology and the industry — commands a significant premium and is even scarcer. An ML engineer who understands credit risk modeling, regulatory requirements, and banking data structures is exponentially more valuable than one who only knows the algorithms.
